Core Clinical Services

Comprehensive care coordination and clinical services across the healthcare continuum

In-Hospital Case Management

Click to learn more

In-Hospital Case Management

What it is: Coordinated clinical oversight during hospital admission, ensuring seamless discharge planning and care continuity.

Who it's for: Patients requiring hospital care with complex discharge needs, post-operative support, or transitional care planning.

How it works: Our case managers work with hospital teams to assess patient stability, plan appropriate discharge pathways, and arrange follow-up care.

Hospital-at-Home Care

Click to learn more

Hospital-at-Home Care

What it is: Hospital-level care delivered safely in a patient's home, providing structured nursing, daily clinical monitoring, and rapid escalation protocols.

Who it's for: Clinically stable patients with acute medical conditions, post-operative recovery needs, or chronic condition exacerbations suitable for home management.

How it works: Registered nurses provide daily visits with vital sign monitoring, clinical assessment, treatment administration, and continuous communication with the treating physician.

Home Infusion Services

Click to learn more

Home Infusion Services

What it is: Safe administration of intravenous therapies in the home environment with trained nursing staff and comprehensive clinical monitoring.

Who it's for: Patients requiring regular infusion therapies including immunoglobulins (IVIG), iron infusions, biologic therapies, and enzyme replacement therapies.

How it works: Certified infusion nurses administer prescribed therapies, monitor for adverse reactions, manage vascular access, and maintain clinical documentation.

Long-Term IV Therapy

Click to learn more

Long-Term IV Therapy

What it is: Extended intravenous treatment courses (4 weeks to 12+ months) delivered safely at home with specialized clinical monitoring and line management.

Who it's for: Patients with chronic infections, bone infections (osteomyelitis), complex fungal infections, or conditions requiring prolonged antimicrobial therapy.

How it works: Specialized nurses manage PICC lines or central lines, administer prescribed antibiotics/antifungals, perform sterile dressing changes, and monitor for complications.

Home Nursing & Clinical Monitoring

Click to learn more

Home Nursing & Clinical Monitoring

What it is: Professional skilled nursing care with structured clinical observation, vital sign monitoring, and continuous communication with treating physicians.

Who it's for: Patients with chronic diseases requiring medication management, wound care, post-operative monitoring, or chronic disease exacerbation support.

How it works: Registered nurses conduct regular home visits, assess clinical stability, monitor treatment adherence, perform wound care, and escalate clinical concerns.

Occupational & Primary Healthcare

What it is: On-site workplace health services including occupational health assessments, preventative care, injury management, and chronic disease screening.

Who it's for: Employers seeking to enhance employee health, reduce absenteeism, manage occupational injuries, and implement preventative health programs.

How it works: Mobile healthcare teams provide on-site assessments, injury management, health screenings, and occupational health surveillance as per regulatory requirements.

Health Screening & Risk Assessment

Who it's for: Individuals and organizations seeking proactive health management, chronic disease early detection, and preventative health planning.

Onsite Disease Management

What it is: Structured disease management programs for chronic conditions including diabetes, hypertension, asthma, and cardiovascular disease.

Who it's for: Patients with chronic diseases requiring ongoing clinical support, medication management, lifestyle counseling, and disease-specific education.

How it works: Clinical coordinators provide patient education, medication monitoring, lifestyle interventions, and coordination with primary care physicians.

Who it's for: Patients seeking convenient access to healthcare professionals without the need for in-person visits, including follow-up care and routine consultations.

How it works: Patients connect with healthcare providers through our secure platform for real-time consultations, prescription management, and health monitoring.

Mental Health Support

What it is: Comprehensive mental health services including counseling, therapy, and support for emotional well-being and mental health conditions.

Who it's for: Individuals experiencing stress, anxiety, depression, or other mental health challenges, seeking professional support and coping strategies.

Palliative Care Services

What it is: Specialized care focused on providing relief from the symptoms and stress of serious illness, improving quality of life for patients and their families.

Who it's for: Patients with serious or life-limiting illnesses, including cancer, heart disease, respiratory conditions, and other chronic progressive diseases.

How it works: Interdisciplinary team provides pain management, symptom control, emotional support, and coordination with primary care physicians and specialists.

Rehabilitation Services

What it is: Comprehensive rehabilitation programs including physical therapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy delivered in the home environment.

Who it's for: Patients recovering from surgery, injury, stroke, or chronic conditions requiring therapeutic interventions to regain independence and function.

How it works: Licensed therapists conduct personalized assessments and develop treatment plans with regular home visits, exercises, and adaptive equipment recommendations.

How Our Services Work

Our standardized care coordination process ensures quality, safety, and clinical efficacy.

1
Referral Received

Doctor, hospital, or medical scheme submits referral with clinical details, patient information, and specific care requirements. Our clinical team acknowledges receipt within 2 hours.

2
Clinical Assessment

CureVida clinical coordinator conducts comprehensive patient assessment to evaluate suitability for home-based care, identify clinical risks, and confirm patient consent.

3
Authorization & Approval

Medical scheme authorization (if required), insurance verification, and approval confirmation. CureVida coordinates directly with administrator for authorization.

4
Care Plan Development

Detailed care plan created based on clinical assessment, physician instructions, and patient preferences. Plan includes visit frequency, monitoring protocols, and escalation procedures.

5
Service Delivery

Qualified healthcare professionals deliver agreed-upon services at home. All patient contacts, clinical observations, and interventions are documented in POPIA-compliant records.

6
Monitoring & Reporting

Continuous clinical monitoring with daily reports to treating physician. Regular updates to medical schemes, family communication, and proactive escalation if clinical concerns arise.
